# Public Safety & Emergency Services CPL — Crosswalk Reference

> **Scope.** Evergreen reference for how emergency-services training (fire, EMS, emergency management, hazardous materials, incident command) maps to California Community College credit through the statewide CPL process. Operational counts defer to the [live dashboards](../current-status/README.md).

## Summary

Public-safety credentials are unusually strong CPL candidates: they are competency-based, standardized by national/state frameworks, carry documented contact hours, and serve a large incumbent workforce already in the field. California faculty have already published statewide CPL credit recommendations across several public-safety series; this note documents the credential landscape, the crosswalk "spine" that converts training into units, existing precedents, the degree destinations, and the accuracy guards that keep the work credible.

## The credit pathway (how a public-safety credential becomes units)

1. A faculty discipline workgroup under **ASCCC Pathways to Credit** reviews the credential's outcomes, contact hours, and assessment.
2. **MAP** builds the credential **exhibit** (outcomes → skills → credit recommendation → local course articulation) and publishes a **statewide credit recommendation** at [map.rccd.edu/statewidecpl](https://map.rccd.edu/statewidecpl/).
3. Colleges **adopt** the recommendation; **faculty determine the credit and the college awards & transcribes it** per **Title 5 §55050** (consistent with the [ACCJC CPL policy](../policy-and-funding/accjc-cpl-policy.md)).
4. Students apply the credit toward certificates, associate degrees, and public-safety baccalaureates.

See the [CPL Implementation Guide](map-cpl-implementation-guide.md) and the [No-CID Courses Crosswalk](no-cid-courses-crosswalk.md) for mechanics; recommendations are *advisory* — the awarding college always makes the final credit decision.

## The credential landscape & crosswalk spine

The cleanest, most stable conversion in California runs:
**FESHE model course → C-ID FIRE descriptor → CCC Fire Technology / EM model curriculum → local AS/certificate → baccalaureate upper division.** FESHE core courses and C-ID FIRE descriptors are both standardized at **3 units / 54 lecture hours**, giving a reliable backbone.

| Framework | What it is / governing body | Credit linkage |
|---|---|---|
| **FESHE** (Fire & Emergency Services Higher Education) | FEMA U.S. Fire Administration / National Fire Academy model curriculum | 6 associate **core** model courses (Principles of Emergency Services, Building Construction, Fire Behavior & Combustion, Fire Prevention, Fire Protection Systems, Safety & Survival), each **≈3 semester credits**; portable via the NFA transcript |
| **FEMA EMI** (Emergency Management Institute) | FEMA, under the National Disaster & Emergency Management University (ACE-accredited) | ~200+ free **Independent Study (IS-###)** courses with IACET CEUs; **EMPP** Basic → Advanced → Executive academies; many courses **ACE-evaluated** (small, often lower-division, frequently bundled) |
| **California SFT** (State Fire Training) | **Office of the State Fire Marshal (OSFM) — within CAL FIRE** *(not Cal OES)* | Certification tracks (Firefighter I/II, Company/Chief/Executive Chief Officer, Instructor). Delivered only by **Accredited Regional Training Programs (ARTPs)** / Accredited Local Academies. Articulates to CCC via FESHE → C-ID FIRE → CCC Fire Technology model curriculum |
| **ACE** (American Council on Education) | National credit-recommendation evaluator; **ACE National Guide**; **ACE Military Evaluations → JST** | Faculty teams recommend semester hours + level + subject; colleges map onto degree requirements. Basis for consistent military/agency CPL |
| **NIMS / ICS / SEMS** | FEMA (NIMS/ICS) + California (SEMS, Gov. Code §8607) | ICS-100/200, IS-700/800 (online); ICS-300/400 (classroom). Credit is **small, often bundled, institution-dependent** — do not assign fixed unit values |
| **CSTI** (California Specialized Training Institute) | Training arm of **Cal OES**, Camp San Luis Obispo, est. 1971; 100+ course titles | Course completion yields a CSTI/Cal OES **state certificate** (not academic units). CSTI-proper courses have no ACE National Guide entry; some regional college-credit arrangements exist today (see precedents) |

### CSTI catalog at a glance (four program series)
- **Hazardous Materials** — First Responder Operations (~16 hrs), **Technician (~160 hrs)**, **Specialist (~80 hrs)**, Incident Commander, Assistant Safety Officer (HAZWOPER / NFPA 472 aligned).
- **Emergency Management** — Emergency Management Specialist Certificate (core + electives), SEMS/NIMS/ICS, **EOC position credentialing**.
- **Criminal Justice / Homeland Security** — Terrorism Specialist track; officer safety / field tactics.
- **Fire & Rescue Training Unit** — delivers FEMA All-Hazards Position Specific, ICS, and OSFM/SFT courses.

## Existing precedents

- **Statewide EMT/EMS credit recommendations** (2024) — adopted by a majority of fire / first-responder colleges. Statewide recommendations also exist for **Paramedic, Firefighter 1, Fire Inspector, and wildland** series.
- **CSTI training already carrying college units** — a regional public-safety training consortium delivers CSTI hazardous-materials courses for college credit, and at least one college embeds CSTI hazmat certifications (General Site Worker, First Responder Operations/Awareness) inside for-credit Environmental Health & Safety courses. A statewide route would standardize and scale these regional arrangements.

## Degree destinations (announced public-safety baccalaureates)

Publicly announced CCC baccalaureates (AB 927 program) that these credits feed:
- **San Diego Miramar College** — B.S. Public Safety Management (approved Oct 2023; launched Fall 2025; CPL/portfolio built into admissions).
- **Moreno Valley College** — B.S. Emergency Management (CCCCO + ACCJC approved; launching Fall 2026).
- **Mission College** — B.S. Emergency Services Administration (launching Fall 2026).
- **College of the Siskiyous** — B.S. Paramedicine.
- **Feather River College** — B.S. Ecosystem Restoration & Applied Fire Management (wildfire / fuels focus).

## Accuracy guards (common errors to avoid)

- **SFT/OSFM is CAL FIRE, not Cal OES.** CSTI (Cal OES) is a separate, complementary lane — it covers the Emergency Management / HazMat / Homeland Security space that SFT (fire) and POST (law enforcement) do not.
- **ACE credit attaches to the FEMA EMI / NFA courses CSTI sometimes delivers**, not to CSTI's own courses. CSTI-proper courses have no ACE National Guide entry — which is why the MAP / ASCCC statewide-recommendation route is the cleaner path for them.
- **Faculty determine credit** (Title 5 §55050); agencies supply training data, colleges award.
- **Do not promise fixed unit values for short ICS / IS courses** — they are small, often bundled, and institution-dependent.
- **Recommendations are advisory** — the awarding college makes the final call.
