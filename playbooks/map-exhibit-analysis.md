# MAP Exhibit Analysis — Analytical Companion

> **Live data supersedes static counts in this overview.** Current articulated credit recommendations, exhibit titles, college counts, and discipline / CPL-type breakdowns are produced by the MAP Exhibit Analysis workbook and reflected on the [MAP CPL Insights Dashboard](https://cpldashboardcccco.azurewebsites.net/insights/dashboard) and the [CPL Project Dashboard](https://cpl-initiative.github.io/cpl-project-tracker/). This file describes the workbook's structure and analytical purpose — refer to the dashboards for current values.

## What this is

The MAP Exhibit Analysis is an Excel workbook that analyzes MAP exhibit data across multiple analytical views. It contains KPI rollups, unique credit-recommendation catalogs, potential articulation opportunity analyses, and breakdowns by discipline, CPL type, mode of learning, college, collaborative status, top exhibits, and student counts.

It is refreshed periodically from MAP platform data. The version-of-record's date appears on its title page.

## What the workbook contains (10 analytical tabs)

### 1. Dashboard KPIs

High-level metrics: total articulated credit recommendations, unique exhibit titles, colleges with articulations. (Current values: dashboards.)

### 2. Unique credit recommendations

Every unique exhibit title with: colleges articulating, total credit recs, unique courses, C-ID status, discipline code, CPL type, mode of learning, collaborative type, originating college, sample course, sample recommendation.

### 3. Potential articulations

Growth-opportunity analysis for each exhibit: colleges already articulating, potential new colleges via C-ID match and TOP-code match, penetration rate, match method. Source data spans the statewide course catalog across all participating colleges.

### 4. By discipline

Distribution of articulated credit recommendations across discipline categories (Engineering & Industrial Technologies, Health, Business, CTE, GE, etc.), including the "unknown / not mapped" share that signals data cleanup opportunities.

### 5. By CPL type

Distribution across standardized assessment, industry certification, portfolio, military JST, credit by exam, and other types.

### 6. By mode of learning

Distribution across self-study / exam prep, industry training, military training, apprenticeships, and other modes.

### 7. By college

Per-college totals including discipline counts, CCC collaborative count, and industry certification count. Identifies leading colleges by volume.

### 8. Collaborative analysis

Distribution between local articulations and statewide / CCC-collaborative articulations.

### 9. Top exhibits

Ranked list of the highest-impact exhibits (those adopted by the most colleges and generating the most recommendations). Examples include POST Basic Academy Certificate (law enforcement) and various industry-recognized credentials.

### 10. Student count by college

College-level eligible-credit counts per student population.

## Strategic value

- The workbook is the analytical companion to MAP platform data, designed to **identify growth opportunities** and **measure articulation penetration** rather than to display current operational counts.
- The "Potential Articulations" tab is the most actionable: it shows where existing exhibits could be adopted by additional colleges based on C-ID and TOP-code matching.
- The "By Discipline" tab's "unknown / not mapped" share is a leading indicator of data-cleanup work needed.
