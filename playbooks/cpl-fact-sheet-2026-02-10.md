# CPL Fact Sheet — February 2026 (Public / Press Edition)

> **Live data supersedes this fact sheet.** Vision 2030 goal-to-date counts and current operational metrics are on the [CPL Project Dashboard](https://cpl-initiative.github.io/cpl-project-tracker/) and the [MAP CPL Insights Dashboard](https://cpldashboardcccco.azurewebsites.net/insights/dashboard). What's preserved here: definitions, sourced research findings, statutory funding amounts, the Vision 2030 goal and stretch goal, and the public resource directory.

## What CPL is

Credit for Prior Learning (CPL) recognizes validated skills gained from military service, industry certifications, apprenticeships, workplace experience, portfolios, and exam-based credit (per Title 5 §55050). The California Community Colleges CPL Initiative scales CPL across all 119 credit and noncredit campuses through the MAP (Mapping Articulated Pathways) platform.

## Vision 2030 — scope

- Vision 2030 sets a statewide CPL beneficiary target and a stretch goal for 2030. Current target, stretch goal, and goal-to-date progress: [CPL Project Dashboard](https://cpl-initiative.github.io/cpl-project-tracker/).
- Available population context: millions of Californians could benefit with optimal CPL capacity. The 2024 Beacon Economics study uses a working-adult-and-veteran population window of ages 25–54 for its modeling.
- MAP is available at no cost to all California Community Colleges.

## Economic impact (Beacon Economics, 2024)

These figures are from the 2024 Beacon Economics study (see [research/economic-impact-2024.md](../research/economic-impact-2024.md)) and represent the projected impact if the Vision 2030 goal is met. They are evergreen until Beacon publishes a refresh.

- **$32.4B** projected combined economic impact at the Vision 2030 statewide CPL beneficiary target
- **$3.7B** immediate savings and preserved educational funds
- **$28.8B** long-term economic benefits over 20 years
- **$14,653 – $26,115** per-student savings at 15 credits, conservative case
- **$1.6B** estimated impact if Veteran Sprint Phase 1 fully succeeds

## Student success deltas (WICHE / CAEL PLA Boost)

Outcome gains from the WICHE / CAEL PLA Boost study, applied to CPL recipients:

- **49% vs 27%** graduation rate — CPL recipients vs non-CPL
- **+25%** overall completion boost
- **+24%** completion boost for Latinx students
- **+14%** completion boost for Black students
- **+19%** completion boost for Pell Grant recipients

## Funding

- **AB 123 (2025, enacted):** $5M ongoing + $15M one-time. First-ever ongoing state CPL funding.
- **FY2026 enacted budget:** additional $2M ongoing + $35M one-time.
- **ESS 25-82 (Dec 2025):** $50,000 local implementation grants to all participating California Community Colleges, distributed February 2026.

For the current funding allocation and spend, see the [CPL Project Dashboard](https://cpl-initiative.github.io/cpl-project-tracker/).
