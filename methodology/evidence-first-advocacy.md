# Evidence-First Advocacy — Lead with the Number

## Framework Overview

Every piece of external CPL communication — legislative advocacy, board memo, press fact sheet, college update, fundraising pitch — leads with a quantified, sourced impact number, and only then explains what produced it. "$32.4B projected economic impact," "49% vs 27% graduation rate," "[X]% of veterans' JSTs uploaded as of today" — these are the tip of the spear. The narrative is in service of the number, not the other way around.

> Operational percentages and counts in this framework are illustrative. For current values, see the [CPL Project Dashboard](../current-status/README.md). Research projections (Beacon Economics, WICHE/CAEL) are sourced and evergreen until the underlying study is refreshed.

## Core Principles

### Principle 1: Lead with the dollar or the percentage, not the story

**Statement:** The first data point in any external artifact is a dollar amount, a percentage, or a headcount tied to a specific outcome. The story follows.

**Evidence:**

- The CPL Initiative public fact sheet opens with "$32.4B projected combined economic impact," "$3.7B immediate savings," "49% vs 27% graduation rate (CPL vs non-CPL)."
- Periodic CPL initiative updates lead with cumulative students served, eligible units, and statewide credit recommendations adopted.
- The AB 123 legislative report opens with student-outcomes and economic-impact tables.
- Weekly tier-based college updates lead with the college's tier (e.g., "Leading — 5/5 criteria") and a key-metrics table.

**Confidence:** High — structural rule across every external artifact.

### Principle 2: Sourced quantification beats compelling prose

**Statement:** Every headline number has a named source and a method footnote. Beacon Economics (2024), WICHE/CAEL PLA Boost, live CCCCO Dashboard — the provenance is visible.

**Evidence:**

- The $32.5B figure is traceable to a 44-page Beacon Economics study with methodology, conservative estimates noted, and data sources (Census ACS, WICHE/CAEL) cited.
- Every number on the public fact sheet links to a specific source document.
- The MAP team operates under an internal rule that mandates a live-data fetch before citing any number in external communication.

**Confidence:** High — explicitly enforced by internal practice.

### Principle 3: Equity deltas are headline material, not footnotes

**Statement:** Outcome gains broken down by underrepresented student populations (Latinx, Black, Pell Grant) are stated as explicit deltas and placed near the top of the artifact. They are not relegated to an appendix.

**Evidence:**

- "+24% completion boost for Latinx students, +14% for Black students, +19% for Pell Grant recipients" appears in the student-success section of the public fact sheet.
- Veteran-specific analysis ($26,115 per student savings at 15 credits) is pulled out as its own category in the economic-impact study, not buried in a table.

**Confidence:** High.

### Principle 4: One aggregate + one per-student figure

**Statement:** Every economic impact section pairs a big aggregate ("$32.5B combined") with a per-student figure ("$14,653–$26,115 savings"). The aggregate makes the case at state level; the per-student figure makes it at human level. Both are needed.

**Evidence:**

- The Beacon Economics study is structured exactly this way: aggregate tables and per-student tables run in parallel.
- "Per-student (15 units): $14,653–$26,115 savings; $115K–$135K 20-year economic output" appears immediately adjacent to "$270M total estimated savings" in periodic updates.

**Confidence:** High.

### Principle 5: Goal-to-date percentage over raw progress

**Statement:** Progress is always expressed as "X of Y (Z%)," never as raw counts alone. Percentages survive audience differences; raw counts don't.

**Evidence:**

- Veteran Sprint goals are all expressed as percentages of targets.
- Periodic updates frame progress as "exceeds 40K goal," "exceeds 85K goal," "84 colleges meeting active participation threshold (original goal was 50)."

**Confidence:** High.

## Applications & Use Cases

### Use Case 1: Drafting any external communication

**When to Apply:** Board memo, press release, legislative briefing, college update, funder pitch.

**How to Apply:**

1. Open with the headline number (dollar, percentage, or headcount).
2. Name the source inline or in a citation footnote.
3. Pair aggregate with per-student figure.
4. Include at least one equity delta near the top.
5. Narrative follows and explains the number.

### Use Case 2: Legislative advocacy

**When to Apply:** Follow-on budget asks; multi-year funding cases.

**How to Apply:**

1. Headline: $32.5B projected 2030 impact.
2. Per-student: $14,653–$26,115 savings.
3. Equity: +24% Latinx, +14% Black, +19% Pell completion boosts.
4. Progress: cumulative students served against the 250K goal; colleges engaged out of 119.
5. Ask: specific dollar amount tied to a specific next-level outcome.

The FY2026 enacted budget added $2M ongoing + $35M one-time to the AB 123 base of $5M ongoing + $15M one-time — the framework was the operational basis for the case.

### Use Case 3: College update (tiered peer comparison)

**When to Apply:** Weekly tier-based college updates.

**How to Apply:**

1. Headline: Tier status (e.g., "Leading — 5/5 criteria").
2. Key metrics table with thresholds.
3. Peer comparison (district or statewide).
4. Gap analysis (what hasn't been adopted that peers have).
5. Recommended actions ranked by impact.

## Boundaries & Limitations

**This framework works when:**

- Audience is external (legislators, media, boards, partners, colleges).
- Underlying data is current and sourced.
- There is a quantifiable outcome to lead with.

**This framework does NOT work when:**

- Audience is internal (teammates) and the number obscures rather than clarifies.
- Data is stale — stale numbers erode credibility faster than a missing number does.
- The underlying program is genuinely at a "set up" phase with no outcomes yet. Use milestones instead.

**Common Pitfalls:**

- Citing stale snapshot figures when live data exists.
- Leading with the headline number but failing to source it.
- Aggregate-only presentation (sounds like state-level PR); per-student-only (sounds anecdotal). Need both.

## Evolution & History

### 2024 — Economic Impact Study commissioned

The Beacon Economics study establishes the $32.5B headline number that everything else hangs on.

### 2024–2025 — Fact sheet and Scaling report codify the figures

Numbers migrate from the study into every downstream artifact.

### 2025 — AB 123 signed

First-ever ongoing state CPL funding. Advocacy used the framework; the framework got its first major legislative validation.

### 2026 — Live-data citation rule formalized

The MAP team formalized the practice as an internal rule: never cite without fetching live data first.
