# Sprint-Based Execution — Bounded Goals, Demonstration-Then-Scale

## Framework Overview

Large statewide initiatives are executed through named, bounded sprints with a small number of measurable goals, a concrete demonstration project, and an explicit scalable model. Sprints provide velocity without losing the long-horizon strategy. "Sprint" here is the CPL program's usage — not Scrum — closer to a time-boxed, outcome-anchored campaign with demonstration + replication baked in.

**Status:** Working (three active sprints running simultaneously; consistent structure)

## Core Principles

### Principle 1: A sprint has 3 goals, not 7

**Statement:** Each sprint names three concrete, measurable goals and tracks them publicly. Seven goals is a strategy; three is a sprint.

**Evidence:**

- The Veteran Sprint explicitly has three goals (JST upload, basic-training credit, statewide JST articulations), each tracked as a public completion percentage.
- The $50K college implementation grants (ESS 25-82) are measured against three priority outcomes, not a longer list.
- The Apprenticeship Sprint has three objectives: degree pathway, stackable credentials, MAP enhancement.

**Confidence:** High — structural rule visible across all three sprints.

### Principle 2: Demonstration project before scalable model

**Statement:** Every sprint includes a specific demonstration project at a specific site, designed to produce the replicable model by a specific date. The demonstration is not the output; the scalable model is.

**Evidence:**

- **29 Palms Demonstration:** Copper Mountain College + MCAGCC, 42 MOS pathways, outcomes by **July 1, 2026**, scalable model for **34 California military bases by March 2027**. Demonstration site, output type, and scaling date are all explicit.
- **Apprenticeship Sprint:** piloting at Santiago Canyon and Norco (construction / IBEW), completion June 2026, designed to scale statewide via DAS data integration.
- **Veteran Sprint Phase 3** is framed as "29 Palms demonstration project — replicable model across 34 CA military bases."

**Confidence:** High — structural rule across sprints.

### Principle 3: Public headline metrics with goal-to-date percentages

**Statement:** Each sprint has 1–2 headline metrics reported as "X of Y (Z%)" rather than raw counts. This makes trend visible and accountability easy.

**Evidence:**

- Veteran Sprint headline metrics are framed as "X of [target] (Z%)" rather than as a raw count.
- Cumulative students served and colleges meeting active-participation thresholds are framed against their original goals so that overperformance and underperformance are equally visible.
- Current values for every sprint's goal-to-date percentages live on the [CPL Project Dashboard](../current-status/README.md), not in this framework file.

**Confidence:** High.

### Principle 4: Sprints nest inside a longer-horizon plan

**Statement:** Sprints are never the whole plan. They sit inside a multi-year roadmap (Vision 2030 / Goal 1 annual milestones) with a terminal target (250K Californians by 2030). The sprint gives velocity; the roadmap gives coherence.

**Evidence:**

- Goal 1 annual milestones: CPL offers scaling 34K → 55K → 90K → 120K → 150K → 250K across 2024-25 to 2029-30. Sprints deliver each year's step.
- Vision 2030 is the enclosing strategic plan. The CPL Workplan is one of several Vision 2030 workplans.

**Confidence:** High.

### Principle 5: Named recognition is part of the sprint

**Statement:** Public recognition of specific colleges and roles ("College of the Year," "MAP Champion") is an execution primitive, not a PR add-on. Recognition creates peer benchmarking and extends culture-pillar reach without central-team cost.

**Evidence:**

- "College of the Year" awards (2023, 2024, 2025) are publicly named with specific outcome metrics in periodic CPL updates.
- Tiered peer comparison ("5 of 5 Leading criteria, X colleges statewide") operationalizes recognition at a weekly cadence.

**Confidence:** Medium — pattern visible, not explicitly articulated as a principle inside any one document.

## Applications & Use Cases

### Use Case 1: Launching a new sprint

**When to Apply:** Any time-bounded campaign within a larger initiative (AI-Ready California year 1, new demonstration project, new state-level advocacy push).

**How to Apply:**

1. Name the sprint.
2. Pick 3 measurable goals, each expressable as "X of Y (Z%)."
3. Identify the demonstration site and the scalable-model target date.
4. Commit to a public progress cadence (weekly tier update, monthly CCCCO memo, spring/fall update).
5. Plan the recognition mechanism (College of the Year, Champion awards, tier system).

### Use Case 2: Diagnosing a stalled sprint

**When to Apply:** Sprint is past its midpoint and goals are behind.

**How to Apply:**

1. Check: is there a demonstration project with a completion date? If no, the sprint is actually just a theme.
2. Check: are headline metrics reported as percentages of a goal, or raw counts? Raw counts hide stagnation.
3. Check: is there a scalable-model artifact (template, playbook, doc) being produced by the demonstration? If no, there's nothing to scale.

### Use Case 3: Deciding what is NOT a sprint

**When to Apply:** Team proposes "let's run a sprint on X."

**How to Apply:** If the work cannot be framed with 3 measurable goals, a demonstration site, a scalable-model date, and a public progress cadence — it is a program or a theme, not a sprint. Rename accordingly.

## Boundaries & Limitations

**This framework works when:**

- Time horizon is 6–24 months.
- There's a natural demonstration site (one college, one district, one base).
- Goal can be quantified and externally visible.

**This framework does NOT work when:**

- Work is fundamentally exploratory (no target state yet).
- Demonstration would compromise the thing being demonstrated (e.g., confidential negotiations).
- Time horizon is <3 months (just ship it) or >3 years (roadmap, not sprint).

**Common Pitfalls:**

- Calling everything a sprint. Sprint inflation dilutes the signal.
- Skipping the scalable-model artifact. Without it, the demonstration is an expensive pilot.
- Too many sprints running simultaneously. Three concurrent is the observed CPL ceiling (Veteran, Apprenticeship, Statewide Credit Recs) — suggests that's near the upper limit.

## Evolution & History

### Fall 2024 — Veteran Sprint launched

First explicit use of the "sprint" framing in the CPL program. Set the structural template.

### 2025–2026 — Two more sprints launched in parallel

Apprenticeship Sprint and Statewide Credit Recommendations Sprint follow the same template. Three concurrent sprints; framework structure consistent across all three.

### 2026 — 29 Palms demonstration project moves to completion

First test of the demonstration-to-scalable-model step at full scale. Outcomes July 1, 2026; scaling target March 2027.
