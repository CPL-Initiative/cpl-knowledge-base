# Expected Economic Benefits of CPL in California — Beacon Economics (June 2024)

> **Beacon's research figures are evergreen** (per-student savings, per-credit economic output, tax-revenue impact) until Beacon publishes a refresh. **Goal-to-date progress against the Vision 2030 target — the population assumption that drives the $32.4B aggregate projection — lives on the [CPL Project Dashboard](../current-status/README.md), not in this file.**

## Summary
Groundbreaking 44-page economic impact study commissioned by the California MAP Initiative and conducted by Beacon Economics. Quantifies the preserved benefits, savings, and positive economic impact of CPL for working adults and veterans across California over a 20-year period. This is the foundational economic case for the CPL Initiative, cited across all major CPL communications.

## Key Findings

### Aggregate Economic Output Increase (20 years)
| CPL Magnitude | Working Adults | Veterans |
|---|---|---|
| 15 Credits | $44B | $630M |
| 30 Credits | $67B | $960M |

### Per-Student Economic Output (20 years)
| CPL Magnitude | Working Adults | Veterans |
|---|---|---|
| 1 Credit | $4,734 | $6,067 |
| 15 Credits | $115K | $135K |
| 30 Credits | $142K | $182K |

### Per-Student Savings
| CPL Magnitude | Working Adults | Veterans |
|---|---|---|
| 15 Credits | $14,653 | $26,115 |
| 30 Credits | $28,936 | $51,905 |

Veterans exhibit almost double the savings of working adults because Post-9/11 GI Bill covers tuition, living costs, and time for up to 36 months.

### Tax Revenue Impact (Annual Average, Aggregate)
| CPL Magnitude | Federal | State | Local & Other |
|---|---|---|---|
| 15 Credits (Working Adults) | $3.65B | $2.01B | $1.25B |
| 30 Credits (Working Adults) | $5.55B | $3.14B | $1.91B |
| 15 Credits (Veterans) | $52.7M | $29.8M | $18M |
| 30 Credits (Veterans) | $80.0M | $45.2M | $27.5M |

### Combined 2030 Goal Projections (Beacon model, at the Vision 2030 statewide CPL target)
- **$32.4B** projected combined economic impact at the Vision 2030 statewide CPL beneficiary target
- **$3.7B** in immediate savings and preserved educational funds
- **$28.8B** in long-term economic benefits over 20 years

## Population Analysis
- **15.9 million** working adults aged 25-54 in California (2022 Census ACS)
- **424,000** military veterans aged 25-54 in California
- 53% of CA working adult population lacks a higher education degree
- ~3 million have attended college but did not earn a degree — prime CPL candidates
- 6.8 million working adults without a college credential could benefit from CPL

### Educational Attainment (Adults 25-54)
- Less than high school: 2.18M (13.7%)
- High school or equivalent: 3.30M (20.7%)
- Some college, no degree: 2.97M (18.7%)
- Associate's degree: 1.16M (7.3%)
- Bachelor's degree: 3.93M (24.7%)
- Master's degree: 1.68M (10.6%)
- Graduate/professional: 0.69M (4.3%)

## Methodology
- Modeled impacts of 15 units (one semester) and 30 units (two semesters) of CPL
- Constrained to Californians ages 25-54
- Conservative estimates used throughout
- Per-unit value estimated from one-thirtieth of the 30-unit impact
- Analyzed: earnings increase, household spending, tax revenue, spillover economic effects
- Sources: U.S. Census ACS, WICHE/CAEL PLA Boost study, Beacon Economics modeling

## Strategic Notes
- The $32.4B figure is the headline number used across CPL communications.
- The per-student savings figures ($14,653–$26,115 for 15 units) are evergreen until Beacon refreshes the model.
- The veteran-specific per-student analysis is the basis for the Veteran Sprint's projected impact.
- Foreword authored by Samuel Lee, Chief Ambassador, California MAP Initiative.
