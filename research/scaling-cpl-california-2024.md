# Scaling Credit for Prior Learning in California — Vision 2030 and the MAP Initiative (October 2024)

> **Live data supersedes static counts in this document.** Goal 1 annual milestones, current colleges participating, statewide credit recommendations adopted, and faculty workgroup progress are tracked on the [CPL Project Dashboard](https://cpl-initiative.github.io/cpl-project-tracker/) and the [MAP CPL Insights Dashboard](https://cpldashboardcccco.azurewebsites.net/insights/dashboard). What's preserved here: the framework, goals, scope, definitions, and historical context.

## Summary

Comprehensive 38-page strategy document authored by Samuel Lee, Ed.D., laying out the vision, goals, implementation strategy, and roadmap for scaling CPL across California's community colleges. Covers MAP Initiative background, three-pillar strategy, Veteran Sprint phases, CPL types and modes, faculty workgroups, platform development, legal framework, equity data, economic impact, and college-level implementation guidance.

## MAP Initiative — three pillars

1. **Culture** — Meet with colleges to develop implementation strategy, provide training, recommend business processes for CPL management. Functions as an extension of local college CPL teams.
2. **Technology** — The MAP platform facilitates CPL creation, faculty approval, adoption, document uploading/parsing, student CPL planning, and college-branded CPL websites displaying implemented opportunities.
3. **Policy** — Convene statewide CPL workgroup, collaborate with colleges on best practices, meet with CSU, UC, and private institutions on transfer alignment, engage interagency partners.

See [Three-Pillar Initiative Design](../methodology/three-pillar-initiative-design.md) for the operating logic distilled from this framing.

## Vision 2030 — three CPL goals

**Goal 1 — Expand CPL and student success.** Serve the Vision 2030 statewide CPL beneficiary target (with a stretch goal) using a target library of statewide credit recommendations developed by faculty/industry workgroups by 2030. Goal 1 includes an annual milestone schedule (CPL offers, statewide recommendations, faculty workgroups convened, institutions processing). Live targets, stretch, and goal-to-date progress: [dashboards](../current-status/README.md).

**Goal 2 — Build a unified statewide system.** CPL-embedded outreach pathways, seamless credit transfer, multi-partner integrations, and a comprehensive Credential Registry.

**Goal 3 — Establish policy and sustainable resources.** Remove regulatory barriers, secure permanent funding, build professional development capacity, and drive best-practice adoption.

## Veteran Sprint — three phases

- **Phase 1** — Systemwide Basic Training credit + JST collection for all 30,000+ enrolled veterans.
- **Phase 2** — AI-assisted JST review through ASCCC faculty workgroups for individual credit recommendations.
- **Phase 3** — 29 Palms demonstration project; replicable model across 34 California military bases.

## CPL types (Title 5 §55050)

Five types: credit by examination; JSTs (military); student portfolios; industry-recognized credentials; standardized exams (AP, IB, CLEP).

## CPL modes / settings

Military training; industry/workplace training; high school articulation; noncredit/ROP/adult education; apprenticeships and internships; proprietary / non-accredited learning; government training; self-study and exam preparation; justice-involved coursework.

## MAP Platform development scope

CPL process in MAP; college implementation of CPL with MAP; CPL Communities of Practice workgroup; college-branded CPL websites; design-build approach; AI in CPL.

## Historical and context points

- MAP originally launched in 2017 as the Military Articulation Platform; expanded in 2020 to all CPL types; integrated into the Chancellor's Office in 2024.
- MAP is offered at no cost to all colleges, with no local IT setup required.
- All course and program data are integrated from the Chancellor's Office Curriculum Inventory (COCI).
- Governor's goal: 70% of working-age Californians hold a postsecondary degree or certificate by 2030.
