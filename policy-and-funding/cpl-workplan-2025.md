# Vision 2030: Credit for Prior Learning Workplan (July 2025)

## Summary
The official Vision 2030 CPL Workplan — a 6-page implementation roadmap prepared by James Todd, Ginni May, and Samuel Lee (with contributions from Anthony Cordova, Craig Hayward, Erin Larson, and Michael "Billy" Wagner). Connects Vision 2030 actions to four concrete CPL activities, each with specific deliverables and outcomes. Published July 2025 (A11Y 7/23/25).

## Vision 2030 Actions Addressed
- **Action 1a:** Scale CPL opportunities focused on military service, apprenticeships, technical industry certifications, and service corps programs
- **Action 5:** Provide flexible course scheduling and CPL opportunities to optimize working learners' timelines

## Three CPL Goals (restated from Scaling CPL)
- **CPL Goal 1:** Expand Equitable Access — the workplan sets a 2030 statewide CPL beneficiary target with a stretch goal, split across working adults/apprentices and veterans. Current targets, stretch goals, and split: see [dashboards](../current-status/README.md).
- **CPL Goal 2:** Build Unified, Interoperable, Student-Centered System — embed CPL in outreach/onboarding/advising, integrate with educational planning and student records, enable comprehensive data tracking
- **CPL Goal 3:** Establish Sustainable Policies, Resources, and Professional Learning — faculty-driven policies, ongoing professional development, scalable tools and partnerships

## Four Workplan Activities

### Activity 1: Build AI-Enhanced CPL Infrastructure
- Streamline/automate CPL workflows from credential-matching to faculty review
- Integrate with CCCApply, MyPath, educational planning, Guided Pathways
- AI-enabled Student CPL Portal serving every California Community Colleges student (annual usage target: dashboards)
- CPL Credential Registry populated with validated credentials (current count: dashboards)
- AI tools for faculty/counselors to review, approve, and guide CPL requests
- Interoperability with Career Passport and student information systems
- **Outcome:** Drives Vision 2030 Actions 1a, 5 and CPL Goals 2, 3

### Activity 2: Convene Faculty-Industry Workgroups
- Faculty-industry workgroups (cross-sector teams) covering target discipline sectors
- Generate the Vision 2030 target volume of statewide CPL credit recommendations
- Validate and document skills across participating college courses
- Create common course crosswalk descriptors for courses without a C-ID or Common Course Number
- Current workgroup count, recommendation count, and crosswalk count: dashboards
- Standardize CPL processes while preserving local faculty oversight
- Inform systemwide CPL repositories, program maps, Career Passport
- **Outcome:** Drives Vision 2030 Actions 1a, 5 and CPL Goal 1

### Activity 3: Build Robust CPL Data Infrastructure
- Finalize CPL MIS codes and reporting standards
- Define KPIs: student access, completion, time-to-degree, cost savings, wage outcomes
- Dashboards and reports for colleges to monitor CPL trends and equity gaps
- Recognize college-level performance and innovation
- **Outcome:** Drives Vision 2030 Actions 1a, 5 and CPL Goal 2

### Activity 4: Coordinate CPL Sprints, Targeted Projects, and Partnerships
- CPL demonstration projects targeting high-impact populations: veterans, apprentices, first responders, allied health, rising scholars, noncredit/adult ed, workforce training, professional licensure (count and status by project: dashboards)
- CPL Rapid Response Team for emerging industries and workforce demands
- Local, regional, and statewide training on CPL policies, faculty-led assessment, crosswalk development
- New and expanded strategic partnerships with industry, state agencies, labor unions, and public/private institutions (current partnership count: dashboards)
- **Outcome:** Drives Vision 2030 Actions 1a, 5 and CPL Goal 1

## Strategic Notes

- The four activities map directly to the dashboard tracking needs identified in internal CPL dashboard-automation planning
- The workplan is the bridge document between Vision 2030's high-level goals and the CPL Initiative's on-the-ground execution
