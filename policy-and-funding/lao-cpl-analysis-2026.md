# Legislative Analyst's Office — CPL Analysis (2026-27 Budget)

> ℹ️ **Operational metrics live on the dashboards, not here.** Specific counts in this mirror reflect the source document's original publication date. See the [CPL Project Dashboard](https://cpl-initiative.github.io/cpl-project-tracker/) and the [MAP CPL Insights Dashboard](https://cpldashboardcccco.azurewebsites.net/insights/dashboard) for current figures.

> **Context.** This summarizes the California Legislative Analyst's Office (LAO) assessment of the Governor's proposed 2026-27 Credit for Prior Learning (CPL) funding, published March 2026 as part of the LAO's annual budget analysis. The LAO is the Legislature's nonpartisan fiscal advisor; it routinely counsels the Legislature to defer new or expanded expenditures until outcome data is available, so a "reject at this time" recommendation is the office's typical posture toward new augmentations. It is mirrored here for **historical and policy context** — to document the funding debate as it stood in 2026.

## Background (as stated by the LAO)

- **CPL is intended to accelerate degree completion.** It awards college credit for skills learned outside college courses — through work, military experience, or other settings (e.g., AP). Research has found CPL students are more likely to complete certificates or degrees, and to do so in less time.
- **CPL takes various forms** — exam, portfolio review, faculty-deemed industry credentials, and military/veteran credit via Joint Services Transcripts (JSTs) and American Council on Education (ACE) recommendations.
- **CCC currently offers CPL on a small scale.** All districts must have a CPL policy, but the Chancellor's Office indicates CPL is not yet implemented at scale, and reliable data is limited. Two data sets for 2024-25 differ substantially: one shows ~40,900 students (2,100 / 5% military-veteran); the other ~17,500 students (8,100 / 46% military-veteran). In both, students earned ~5-7 units on average (about two courses).
- **Funding to date:** $6M one-time in 2024-25 (administered jointly with the Riverside Community College District); then $15M one-time + $5M ongoing in 2025-26, including $5.9M in local implementation grants at $50,000 per college and roughly 15 RCCD-based staff positions. Colleges receiving grants commit to (1) awarding CPL through JSTs, (2) implementing systemwide credit recommendations, and (3) strengthening local procedures to identify eligible students. Outcome reports are due March 1, 2026 (on the 2024-25 funds) and January 31, 2028 (on the 2025-26 funds).

## The Governor's proposal

On top of the prior two budgets, the Governor's 2026-27 budget proposed **$35M one-time and $2M ongoing** Proposition 98 General Fund for CPL. The Chancellor's Office indicated the ongoing funds, combined with 2025-26 ongoing funds, would fully cover operating costs for the systemwide initiative, and that reserved and new one-time funds would go to additional local implementation grants allocated on CPL outcomes.

## The LAO's assessment

- **May not address the key barriers.** The system lacks consistent rules for equating experience to credit; faculty work-group recommendations are still under development and adoption is uncertain; colleges may lack the staff or technology to implement. Small, largely one-time funding may not resolve the underlying barriers to scaling.
- **Outcomes of prior rounds are not yet known.** The report due January 31, 2028 is expected to cover progress on credit recommendations, eligible-student identification, and technology infrastructure. Until then, the Legislature lacks complete CPL data and evidence of impact on completion and time-to-degree.
- **Existing funding remains available.** Prior rounds could cover the initiative's operating costs through 2029-30 and local grants through 2027-28. Broader categoricals can also support CPL — the Student Equity and Achievement Program ($524M ongoing) and the Strong Workforce Program ($290M ongoing), the latter explicitly encouraging CPL-applied workforce training.

## The LAO's recommendation

**Reject the proposal at this time.** The LAO recommends waiting for the statutorily required outcome report (due by January 31, 2028) before providing additional CPL funding, after which the Legislature could reassess scalability and appropriate funding levels. Even without new funding, the system would continue implementing CPL using prior rounds.
