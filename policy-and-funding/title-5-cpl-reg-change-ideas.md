# Title 5 CPL Reg Change Ideas

**Note:** Below are regulatory and policy reforms that would help scale CPL opportunities and awards and improve equal access to credit across the system. Each item needs to be researched to note ed code basis and regulation impacts. Would be helpful to illustrate below changes by using a learner scenario to show the outcomes of the changes.

## Proposed Regulatory and Policy Reforms

1. **T5 55050:** Clarify/authorize CPL awards for Course, GE Area, Elective

2. **T5 55050:** CPL Reciprocity between colleges
   - Colleges recognize all transcribed CPL from other CCCs and apply credit to students' programs without secondary review.
   - Colleges adopt or adapt statewide credit recommendations for local course credit--ensuring equal access to CPL opportunities for students throughout the system.

3. **T5 55050, 55022:** CR CPL notation rather than P/NP
   - 55022 - Pass-No Pass Grading Option: https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/california/5-CCR-55022
     - (a) The policy adopted pursuant to section 55021 may permit courses to be offered in either or both of the following categories and, if so, shall require that each college catalog specify the category into which each course falls:
       1. Courses in which all students are evaluated on a "pass-no pass" or "credit-no credit" basis.
       2. Courses in which each student may elect until the last day of instruction, as established and published by the districts, whether the basis of evaluation is to be "pass-no pass" or a letter grade.
     - (b) All units of credit earned on a "pass-no pass" or "credit-no credit" basis in accredited California institutions of higher education or equivalent out-of-state institutions shall be counted in satisfaction of community college curriculum requirements.
     - (c) Units earned on a "pass-no pass" or "credit-no credit" basis shall not be used to calculate grade point averages. However, units attempted for which a student receives a "NP" designation (as defined in section 55023) shall be considered in probation and dismissal procedures.

4. **T5 55050:** Recommend no local limit on CPL units

5. **Ed Code/T5 55050:** CVC CPL awarding by central body

6. **Ed Code?/T5 55050:** Honor ASCCC statewide credit recommendations (TBL 2026 draft)

7. **Ed Code/T5 58161:** Add CPL option through apportionment
   - Title 5, § 58161 - Apportionment for Credit Course Enrollment
   - A community college district may claim state apportionment for students enrolled in credit courses on the district's census date [or receive credit for prior learning], except as otherwise indicated in this section.

8. **High School CPL:** Counts toward dual enrollment

9. Remove requirement to notate CPL on transcript

10. Require colleges to transcribe all CPL, including waived coursework or GE area credit and elective units applied to satisfy local degree unit requirments.

11. Require CPL course, program, and student data to be documented on CO CPL infrastructure MAP platform. Support the development of a data integrity and integration process that ensures MAP and MIS data are aligned and accurate.

12. Clarify that CPL need not be listed on CORs (all courses are eligible at the discretion of discipline faculty)

13. Implement Common Course Crosswalk through ASCCC Pathways to Credit to ensure equitable access for learners to systemwide CPL

14. Require skills to be identified and verified for all CPL

15. CPL denials shall be recorded along with a justification

16. Colleges shall consider and where possible honor the CPL credit recommendations in MAP (aligns with T5 statement to honor ACE CRs)
