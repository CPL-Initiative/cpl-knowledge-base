# 2026 CPL Initiative Report (AB 123 Legislative Report)

> Filed pursuant to **AB 123** (Chaptered, July 2025) — see the [official chaptered version of the bill](https://map.rccd.edu/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/07292025-AB-123-Chaptered-Version.pdf).

> **Live data supersedes this mirror.** Specific student counts, units, savings, and goal-to-date percentages in this report reflect March 1, 2026 — the snapshot date of the legislative filing. For current figures and goals, see the [CPL Project Dashboard](https://cpl-initiative.github.io/cpl-project-tracker/) and the [MAP CPL Insights Dashboard](https://cpldashboardcccco.azurewebsites.net/insights/dashboard). This file preserves the report's structure, framing, and qualitative findings.

## Summary

The official AB 123 legislative report submitted to the California State Senate by Chancellor Sonya Christian (dated March 26, 2026). Covers the full scope of CPL Initiative progress: student outcomes, technology infrastructure, faculty workgroups, partnerships, data collection plans, barriers, and a multi-year budget through 2029-30.

## Scope of student outcomes covered

The report documents student outcomes from the MAP platform across three populations — military / veteran, working (non-military), and apprentice — broken down by:

- Eligible units (articulated and offered) vs transcribed units (recorded on student transcripts)
- CPL type: military JSTs, industry certifications, portfolio, standardized assessments (AP/IB/CLEP), credit by exam
- Academic discipline: CTE/health/business, general education, electives
- Implementation tier of the awarding college

For current values of every count in the report's Tables 1.2 through 1.5, see the dashboards.

## Economic impact

Estimated savings and 20-year projected economic impact are calculated from the platform's eligible-unit and transcribed-unit totals applied to the 2024 Beacon Economics model. Per-student savings range from $14,653 to $26,115 (15 credits, conservative case). At the Vision 2030 statewide CPL beneficiary target, the Beacon model projects $32.4B in combined statewide economic impact by 2030. Current target and stretch goal: see [dashboards](../current-status/README.md).

Current aggregate immediate savings and 20-year impact: see dashboards.

## Implementation status

The report uses three implementation states for the 116 credit colleges plus 3 noncredit programs:

1. **Implementation stage** — 5+ approved articulations, 10+ documented students in MAP, and a configured CPL landing page listing approved articulations and a primary CPL contact.
2. **Growth** — colleges beyond the implementation stage with continued expansion of articulations, recommendations, and student volume.
3. **Engaged / not yet engaged** — colleges working toward implementation or not yet active on the platform.

Current college counts in each state: see dashboards.

## Progress toward meeting objectives

### Systemwide process for student identification and notification

- Dynamic CPL landing pages and the MAP CPL Dashboard automatically display approved CPL opportunities to the public based on prior learning credentials.
- CCCApply integration identifies military students for JST processing.
- The **Student CPL Portal** is scheduled for **May 2026** production release. Phase 2 (December 2026) will integrate Program Pathways Mapper, CVC course offerings, and a resume analyzer/builder.

### Systemwide process to award credits

- The MAP platform serves as the systemwide one-stop resource for colleges to create and share faculty-determined CPL articulations.
- Following the 2024 statewide credit recommendations for EMT/EMS, 15 of 24 colleges have adopted the recommendations for their fire and first-responder pathways.
- Student CPL Plans document the offer, acceptance, application to degree pathways, transfer destinations, and transcription per Title 5 §55050(k).
- Technical assistance is delivered through the CPL Implementation Guide, regional CPL training events (South, Central, North), by-request technical assistance to regional consortia, office hours, and IEPI Partnership Resource Team support.

### Systemwide technology infrastructure

The MAP platform supports all stages of the CPL lifecycle:

- Public outreach: college CPL Landing sites, MAP CPL Dashboard, CPL on Program Pathways Mapper, CVC course offerings, the forthcoming Student CPL Portal, and the planned California Credential Registry
- Document ingestion: AI-enabled processing of JSTs, industry certifications, portfolios, credit by exam records, and standardized exam records
- Student CPL Plan: offer, acceptance, application to degree pathways, transcription
- Exhibit creation: outcomes, skills, credit recommendations, local course articulations for non-college credentials

**Two AI-assisted projects are underway:**

1. **AI certification-to-course matching** — Phase 1 covered industry certifications in priority sectors; Phase 2 beta is expanding the pool, with production release planned for **June 2026**.
2. **AI apprenticeship CPL tools** — Phase 1 supports apprenticeship and journey-worker CPL, degree-progress reports, and outreach, beginning with construction trades and electricians at Santiago Canyon College and Norco College. Phase 2 will scale statewide in collaboration with trade partners.

A **statewide faculty workgroup portal** is planned for May 2026, enabling ASCCC Pathways to Credit workgroups to develop statewide credit recommendations, validate skills and credentials, and create common-course crosswalks for courses without a C-ID or Common Course Number.

MAP CPL data interoperability is being implemented through Credential Engine's Credential Transparency Description Language (CTDL) tags.

### Convening faculty discipline workgroups

The Chancellor's Office and the MAP team have partnered with ASCCC to convene statewide faculty workgroups under the CPL Workplan, targeting discipline-sector certification analysis and statewide credit recommendations. AI-assisted skills matching to course outcomes is underway to support faculty evaluation.

Current count of workgroups, recommendations, and credential exhibits: see dashboards.

### Leveraging partnerships

Active partnerships listed in the report:

- **California Labor and Workforce Development Agency**, **California Workforce Development Boards**, **Cradle to Career** — Career Passport and Credential Registry, workforce readiness, credential transparency.
- **Department of Apprenticeship Standards (DAS)** — apprenticeship data for the Apprenticeship Sprint.
- **Futuro Health** — Behavioral Health CPL for alumni and current trainees.
- **Public Works Alliance EMS Corps** — CPL pathways for EMT and Paramedic training, leading to healthcare careers and baccalaureate pathways in Emergency Management Services.
- **California Joint Apprenticeship Committee (Cal-JAC)** — certification-based credit recommendations for first responder apprentices; ~14,000 first responder trainees.
- **Iron Workers union** — apprenticeship competencies aligned with coursework at Cerritos College.
- **Department of Developmental Services** — Adult Care training for individuals with intellectual disabilities.
- **Credential Engine** — CPL data integrated with Credential Registry infrastructure via CTDL.
- **WestEd** — interagency coordination, skills/credential cataloguing, Credential Registry development, eTranscript and Career Passport integration.
- **California Department of Education** — skills alignment with the California Advanced CTE Framework.

### Intersegmental collaboration

- **CSU** — exploratory meetings on MAP functionality, transcribed-CPL acceptance, and Cal-GETC (which currently accepts only credit by examination).
- **UC** — discussions on CPL policy, Cal-GETC, and potential data-share agreements.
- **Private institution partners** — transfer policy and procedure collaboration; the Counselor Resources Hub publishes the top 10 out-of-state and private institution policies.

Transfer acceptance currently varies by segment and method of assessment.

## Observations

- The CPL infrastructure has facilitated credit pathways across **thousands of unique course titles in hundreds of subject areas** (specific totals: dashboards).
- **Inverse relationship** between articulation volume and student impact: military articulations comprise a large share of records but a smaller share of students served, while standardized assessments are a small share of articulations but drive a disproportionately large share of student volume. This pattern reflects the Veteran Sprint's first-phase focus on JST uploads, setting up Phase 2 (individual articulation) and Phase 3 (29 Palms demonstration).
- Industry certifications and credit-by-exam pathways show strong student-to-articulation efficiency.
- A significant share of articulations remain unactivated by students — flagged in the report as the deepest near-term growth opportunity.

## Plan for improving data collection

- CPL transcription and linking with existing data elements were historically not prioritized; Title 5 changes in 2020 and MAP investments make MAP↔MIS reconciliation necessary, with full alignment scheduled for 2027.
- The MIS Data Element Dictionary SY domain was revised this academic year to align with current CPL practices, Title 5 changes, and Vision 2030.
- MAP–MIS reconciliation procedures and tools are scheduled for release at the end of 2026.
- Enhanced technical assistance is being delivered through IEPI PRT and via direct regional and local training (8 large-scale trainings in the last 2 academic years, 1,500+ faculty/staff/administrators introduced to current CPL procedures).

## Remaining barriers to systemwide expansion

**Institutional level:**

- Dedicated staffing shortages
- Misconceptions about CPL
- Professional development gaps for counselors, articulation officers, and admissions/records evaluators
- Proprietary local CPL systems and SIS systems not yet connected to MAP
- Inconsistent or absent CPL transcription procedures
- Local implementation funding gaps and lack of sustainable CPL funding within the Student-Centered Funding Formula

**Systemic level:**

- Data silos among Chancellor's Office systems, related agency systems, and intersegmental partners
- MIS reporting procedures for general-education-area credit and elective awards require new systemwide procedures
- Transfer inconsistencies across intersegmental partners
- Additional technology functionality needed for long-term interoperability (eTranscript, CCCApply, Credential Registry, Career Passport)
- Systemwide student identification codes not yet integrated in MAP; MAP and MIS student data alignment required for Career Passport / Credential Registry integration with Labor and Workforce identifiers

## Expenditure plan for appropriated funds

- The 2024 **$6 million** one-time appropriation (Provision 2 of Item 6870-488) supported statewide CPL pathway mapping. Fully allocated as of March 1, 2026 (Table 1.1).
- **FY 2025-26:** **$5 million ongoing + $15 million one-time** (under AB 123). Of the $15M one-time, **$5.9M was distributed in February 2026** as **$50,000 local implementation grants** to 118 colleges and noncredit programs. College of Sequoias was the only campus to decline. Grant conditions are documented in [ESS 25-82](ess-25-82-funding-memo.md).
- **AI Tools for Apprentices** (Santiago Canyon + Norco): **$54,764** supplementing the $1,345,236 project funding from the $6M one-time appropriation. Designed to scale statewide if it proves effective during beta.
- The remaining $9,045,236 of the FY25-26 one-time is allocated for 2026–2029 to cover the anticipated $2M annual additional funding and $5M ongoing support for CPL infrastructure operations, technology, sprints, projects, and systemwide technical assistance — a precaution if the $2M ongoing addition is not approved for 2026. The estimated annual cost for CPL infrastructure, college support, and public access is approximately $7–7.5M, varying with special-project scale.
- **FY2026 enacted budget:** additional **$2M ongoing + $35M one-time** (referenced as proposed in the report; subsequently enacted).

For full multi-year budget detail (Table 1.6 and underlying allocations), see the official PDF. Live workplan allocation and spend rollups appear on the [CPL Project Dashboard](https://cpl-initiative.github.io/cpl-project-tracker/).

## Conclusion

The initiative has laid a durable foundation through the MAP platform, faculty workgroups, AI-assisted tools, regional trainings, sprints, and technical assistance. The Student CPL Portal, statewide faculty workgroup portal, and expanded AI functionality scheduled for 2026 are expected to accelerate access and equitable outcomes.

Significant barriers remain — staffing shortfalls, data silos, inconsistent transcription, regulatory constraints, transfer uncertainties — and addressing them requires sustained investment, intersegmental collaboration, and the full realization of in-progress technology integrations across MAP, MIS, CCCApply, the California Credential Registry, and the Career Passport.

The Chancellor's Office position is that the $2M ongoing and $35M one-time addition (subsequently enacted for FY2026) is critical to achieving the Vision 2030 statewide CPL beneficiary target and the corresponding $32.4B projected combined economic impact by 2030.
