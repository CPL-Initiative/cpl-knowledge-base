## Summary

The official AB 123 legislative report submitted to the California State Senate by Chancellor Sonya Christian (dated March 26, 2026). Covers the full scope of CPL Initiative progress: student outcomes, technology infrastructure, faculty workgroups, partnerships, data collection plans, barriers, and a multi-year budget through 2029-30.

The report frames the initiative as supporting **Vision 2030**, the **CPL Workplan**, and the **2025 California Master Plan for Career Education**, with the faculty credit-recommendation work feeding the forthcoming California Credential Registry and students' Career Passports.

## Filed snapshot (March 1, 2026)

The figures the report filed with the Legislature, preserved here **only as the historical record of the filing** (current numbers: dashboards):

- **42,820 students** documented on the MAP platform with faculty-approved CPL opportunities — 20,845 military, 22,045 non-military working, 692 apprentices — averaging 4.3 eligible units per student
- **~93,000 units transcribed** (average 2.1 per student)
- **$269M** estimated immediate savings and **$1.06B** projected 20-year economic impact (Beacon Economics model, based on eligible units)
- **53 of 116 colleges** at the implementation stage; 96 colleges showing some level of platform effort
- **11 of 25** targeted faculty discipline workgroups convened (40 planned by 2030); **235 of 1,000** Vision 2030 statewide credit recommendations documented across **101 credential exhibits** approved or pending
- CPL landing pages configured at all 116 colleges

## Scope of student outcomes covered

The report documents student outcomes from the MAP platform across three populations — military / veteran, working (non-military), and apprentice — broken down by:

- Eligible units (articulated and offered) vs transcribed units (recorded on student transcripts)
- CPL type: military JSTs, industry certifications, portfolio, standardized assessments (AP/IB/CLEP), credit by exam
- Academic discipline: CTE/health/business, general education, electives
- Implementation tier of the awarding college

For current values of every count in the report's Tables 1.2 through 1.5, see the dashboards.

### How to read the numbers (report's own data notes)

- **Eligible (awarded) units** are units articulated by the college and offered to the student; **transcribed units** are those the college has marked as recorded on the transcript.
- **Population and CPL-type totals vary slightly from headline totals** because some students are eligible under more than one CPL type (Table 1.4 note). Breakdowns therefore do not sum exactly to the deduplicated student count.
- **Transcription marking is the newest local practice.** Few colleges had begun marking units transcribed in MAP as of the filing; the counselor offer/accept/appeal flow per Title 5 §55050(k) is being trained statewide this year, and the report expects transcribed units to rise through the end of 2026. Until then, the report states that **eligible units are the more reliable indicator** of the units students have likely received (Table 1.3 note).

## Economic impact

Estimated savings and 20-year projected economic impact are calculated from the platform's eligible-unit and transcribed-unit totals applied to the 2024 Beacon Economics model. ## Progress toward meeting objectives

### Systemwide process for student identification and notification

- Dynamic CPL landing pages and the MAP CPL Dashboard automatically display approved CPL opportunities to the public based on prior learning credentials.
- CCCApply integration identifies military students and facilitates their authorization for colleges to obtain Joint Services Transcripts (JSTs) for CPL evaluation.
- The Veteran Sprint's first goal drove **more than 20,000 JSTs uploaded to the MAP platform**; building on it, the Apprenticeship Sprint (supported by DAS) is working to document all registered California community college apprentices, with new agency landing pages (Cal-JAC first) curating public access for ~14,000 first responder trainees.
- The **Student CPL Portal** is scheduled for **May 2026** production release. Phase 2 (December 2026) will integrate Program Pathways Mapper, CVC course offerings, and a resume analyzer/builder.

### Systemwide process to award credits

- The MAP platform serves as the systemwide one-stop resource for colleges to create and share faculty-determined CPL articulations.
- Following the 2024 statewide credit recommendations for EMT/EMS, 15 of 24 colleges have adopted the recommendations for their fire and first-responder pathways. Darren Hall (Miramar College, fire workgroup co-chair) is quoted on students using CPL to complete an AS degree and qualify for Miramar's newly approved Bachelor's in Public Safety Management: *"CPL creates an efficient and meaningful pathway from workforce achievement to advanced higher education opportunities."*
- Student CPL Plans document the offer, acceptance, application to degree pathways, transfer destinations, and transcription per Title 5 §55050(k).
- Technical assistance is delivered through the CPL Implementation Guide, regional CPL training events (South, Central, North), by-request technical assistance to regional consortia (LARC, IEDRC, OCRC, BACCC, SCCCD, LACCD), office hours, and IEPI Partnership Resource Team support — including PRT assistance and funding for West Los Angeles, Palomar, Bakersfield, Coalinga, East Los Angeles, and Los Angeles Trade Tech.

### Systemwide technology infrastructure

The MAP platform supports all stages of the CPL lifecycle:

- Public outreach: college CPL Landing sites, MAP CPL Dashboard, CPL on Program Pathways Mapper, CVC course offerings, the forthcoming Student CPL Portal, and the planned California Credential Registry
- Document ingestion: AI-enabled processing of JSTs, industry certifications, portfolios, credit by exam records, and standardized exam records
- Student CPL Plan: offer, acceptance, application to degree pathways, transcription
- Exhibit creation: outcomes, skills, credit recommendations, local course articulations for non-college credentials

**Two AI-assisted projects are underway:**

1. **AI certification-to-course matching** — Phase 1 is complete, covering **117 industry certifications across 33 priority-sector disciplines**; Phase 2 beta is expanding the pool, with production release planned for **June 2026**. (The filed v1.1 PDF prints 107 in Table 1.1 and 117 in the technology section; the MAP team has confirmed 117 is correct, with a report correction planned.)
2. **AI apprenticeship CPL tools** — Phase 1 supports apprenticeship and journey-worker CPL, degree-progress reports, and outreach, beginning with construction trades and electricians at Santiago Canyon College and Norco College. Phase 2 will scale statewide in collaboration with trade partners.

A **statewide faculty workgroup portal** is planned for May 2026, enabling ASCCC Pathways to Credit workgroups to develop statewide credit recommendations, validate skills and credentials, and create common-course crosswalks for courses without a C-ID or Common Course Number.

MAP CPL data interoperability is being implemented through Credential Engine's Credential Transparency Description Language (CTDL) tags.

### Convening faculty discipline workgroups

The Chancellor's Office and the MAP team have partnered with ASCCC to convene statewide faculty workgroups under the CPL Workplan, targeting discipline-sector certification analysis and statewide credit recommendations. AI-assisted skills matching to course outcomes is underway to support faculty evaluation.

Current count of workgroups, recommendations, and credential exhibits: see dashboards.

## Observations

As filed (March 1, 2026 data):

- The CPL infrastructure has facilitated credit pathways across **2,223 unique course titles in 403 subject areas** (~250 distinct areas after local consolidation), representing 59,230 total eligible credit units.
- **Inverse relationship** between articulation volume and student impact: military articulations comprise **78.2% of all records but 17.6% of students served** (7,226), while standardized assessments are just **5.6% of articulations but drive 34.4% of student volume** (14,156). The report attributes this to the Veteran Sprint's first-phase focus on JST uploads — a new procedure that sets colleges up for goals 2 and 3 (articulating and offering CPL from the uploaded JSTs) this year.
- Industry certifications and credit-by-exam pathways show strong student-to-articulation efficiency: together **18,909 students across 2,941 articulations**.
- A significant share of articulations remain unactivated by students — only **11.9% of all articulations** had been activated, and 22 of 117 participating colleges reported zero student utilization — flagged in the report as the deepest near-term growth opportunity.
- By academic field, 40% of articulations fall in CTE/health/business, 35% in general education, 25% in electives; by method of assessment, 60% involve certifications or licenses, 25% military transcripts, 10% portfolios and exams, 5% other.

## Plan for improving data collection

- CPL transcription and linking with existing data elements were historically not prioritized; Title 5 changes in 2020 and MAP investments make MAP↔MIS reconciliation necessary, with full alignment scheduled for 2027.
- The MIS Data Element Dictionary SY domain was revised this academic year to align with current CPL practices, Title 5 changes, and Vision 2030.
- MAP–MIS reconciliation procedures and tools are scheduled for release at the end of 2026.
- Enhanced technical assistance is being delivered through IEPI PRT and via direct regional and local training (8 large-scale trainings in the last 2 academic years, 1,500+ faculty/staff/administrators introduced to current CPL procedures).

## Remaining barriers to systemwide expansion

**Institutional level:**

- Dedicated staffing shortages
- Misconceptions about CPL
- Professional development gaps for counselors, articulation officers, and admissions/records evaluators
- Proprietary local CPL systems and SIS systems not yet connected to MAP
- Inconsistent or absent CPL transcription procedures
- Local implementation funding gaps and lack of sustainable CPL funding within the Student-Centered Funding Formula

**Systemic level:**

- Data silos among Chancellor's Office systems, related agency systems, and intersegmental partners
- MIS reporting procedures for general-education-area credit and elective awards require new systemwide procedures
- Transfer inconsistencies across intersegmental partners
- Additional technology functionality needed for long-term interoperability (eTranscript, CCCApply, Credential Registry, Career Passport)
- Systemwide student identification codes not yet integrated in MAP; MAP and MIS student data alignment required for Career Passport / Credential Registry integration with Labor and Workforce identifiers

## Conclusion

The initiative has laid a durable foundation through the MAP platform, faculty workgroups, AI-assisted tools, regional trainings, sprints, and technical assistance. The Student CPL Portal, statewide faculty workgroup portal, and expanded AI functionality scheduled for 2026 are expected to accelerate access and equitable outcomes.

Significant barriers remain — staffing shortfalls, data silos, inconsistent transcription, regulatory constraints, transfer uncertainties — and addressing them requires sustained investment, intersegmental collaboration, and the full realization of in-progress technology integrations across MAP, MIS, CCCApply, the California Credential Registry, and the Career Passport.

The Chancellor's Office position is that the $2M ongoing and $35M one-time addition (subsequently enacted for FY2026) is critical to achieving the Vision 2030 statewide CPL beneficiary target and the corresponding $32.4B projected combined economic impact by 2030.
