# Veterans Sprint Plan

A public summary of the California Community Colleges' **Veterans Sprint** — a systemwide initiative, led by the Chancellor's Office (CCCCO) through the MAP Initiative at Riverside Community College District, to ensure California's enrolled service members (veterans, active duty, reservists, national guard) receive college credit for military service through the **Joint Services Transcript (JST)**.

## Quick Summary

Launched in **Fall 2024**, the Veterans Sprint is organized around three strategic goals: (1) upload all enrolled service members' JSTs into the MAP platform; (2) award appropriate course credit for **basic military service** ("Boot Camp"); and (3) articulate JST **American Council on Education (ACE) Credit Recommendations** across the system for additional CPL opportunities.

The plan shifts the Sprint from an implementation phase to an ongoing one, targeting full Goal 1 completion in 2026/2027, course-credit compliance at all 116 colleges by 2027/2028, and a documented, scalable **base/college model** — anchored by the Copper Mountain College / 29 Palms (MCAGCC) demonstration project — ready for deployment across California's 34+ military installations by 2030.

Its organizing principle: **the work happens at the colleges**, so every goal pairs a deliverable with the technical assistance, professional development, and infrastructure that makes the deliverable achievable for college staff, faculty, and administrators.

## Why This Matters

- **A clear, repeatable model.** The 29 Palms / Copper Mountain demonstration — plus active base engagement at Camp Pendleton and planned outreach to Fort Irwin — is a base/college template ready for replication across California's military installations, positioning the CCC system as a national leader in military CPL.
- **Aligned infrastructure already in place.** Title 5 updates, CCCApply OPT-IN modifications, statewide MOUs, the Basic Training Exhibit, and the Most Common ACE CRs table mean colleges no longer have to build the scaffolding — they use it.
- **Veteran-centered outcomes.** Converting earned military learning into transcripted college credit reduces time-to-degree, preserves G.I. Bill entitlement, and lowers cost for military-connected learners.

## The Three Strategic Goals

### Goal 1 — Upload all enrolled service members' JSTs into the MAP platform
*Support staff with bulk-order pathways and individualized technical assistance.* Close the remaining gap to all enrolled service members through (1) individualized technical assistance to colleges still In-Progress or Stationary, (2) a CCCCO bulk-order pathway via CCCApply OPT-IN, and (3) continued professional development on bulk-ordering for colleges onboarding to the JST Portal. A **Department of War partnership** (close to finalizing) would enable the CCCCO to order JSTs on students' behalf for all 116 colleges — converting Goal 1 from a per-college lift to a system-level service.

### Goal 2 — Award appropriate course credit for basic military service
*Support faculty with curriculum tools, the Statewide Exhibit (Kinesiology/Health), and professional development.* Convert colleges still using waive/clear/satisfy designations to actual course credit in a Health course and a Kinesiology Activity course, anchored by the **CPL Statewide Collaborative DoD-Basic-Training-CPL Exhibit**, in coordination with discipline Kinesiology/Health faculty.

### Goal 3 — Articulate JST ACE Credit Recommendations system-wide
*Support staff, faculty, administrators, and credentialed users with implementation infrastructure and professional development.* Expand ACE CR articulation toward all 116 colleges, prioritizing the **Most Common ACE CRs** table for highest student-credit yield, with MAP providing college demonstrations and trainings, ACE Military Guide / JST CR professional development, and 1:1 implementation support.

## Systemic Barriers Removed

- **Title 5 updates** — §55050 and §55524 advanced to reduce CPL barriers.
- **CCCApply OPT-IN** — modified (Summer 2025) to streamline JST authorization at intake.
- **Standardized JST Operations procedures** — distributed to colleges for access via JST.DODED.MIL.
- **Systemwide MOUs** — distributed and made available to all CCCs for use of the MAP platform.
- **Basic Training Exhibit** — the CPL Statewide Collaborative DoD-Basic-Training-CPL Exhibit (9/19/25) published as the faculty reference.
- **Most Common ACE CRs table** — released to focus faculty review effort on highest-yield articulations.

## The Demonstration-Project Model — 29 Palms / Copper Mountain College

The MCAGCC–Copper Mountain College partnership is the embedded base/college demonstration within the Veterans Sprint: an on-base visit targeted for **Veterans Day, November 2026**; CPL pathways for the installation's Military Occupational Specialties (MOS) by 2028; and a scalable model ready for deployment across California's 34+ military installations.

Each base visit follows the same template: introduce the MAP platform, map the installation's predominant MOS to ACE Credit Recommendations and college courses, and stand up a local upload-and-articulate workflow. Because it is a strategic priority in its own right, the demonstration project is governed by **its own plan** as a distinct component of the Sprint.

## Partnerships and Base Engagement

Beyond the embedded 29 Palms / Copper Mountain demonstration, the Sprint builds direct partnerships with California military installations and veteran-serving agencies:

- **Military base visits** — on-site engagement with base education officers, transition staff, and command leadership (e.g., **Camp Pendleton** underway; **Fort Irwin** targeted), feeding a documented, scalable base/college playbook anchored by 29 Palms.
- **CalVet / CalTAP engagements** — participation in California Department of Veterans Affairs (CalVet) California Transition Assistance Program (CalTAP) events (in person and virtual), walking transitioning service members and higher-education professionals through the benefits-to-credit pathway: Educational Benefits → G.I. Bill → Joint Services Transcript (JST) → Credit for Prior Learning (CPL).

## Governance Model

The Veterans Sprint is the **master project**, delivered through distinct **components** that each carry their own micro-RACI, so role clarity is maintained at both the master and component levels. The Military Base Demonstration Project (29 Palms / Copper Mountain College, expanding to Camp Pendleton and Fort Irwin) is the first such component; additional components can be added under the same structure as the Sprint scales. The master-project RACI sets system-level accountability across the three goals; each component micro-RACI governs that component and its own goals. *(Specific role assignments are maintained internally and are not part of this public summary.)*

**RACI role definitions used across the plan:**

| Role | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Responsible (R)** | Central contact with authority to lead and represent the work; coordinates stakeholders and engages the Accountable party at key moments. |
| **Accountable (A)** | Owns the outcome and decisions; signs off / approves; the person to whom "R" is accountable. |
| **Consulted (C)** | Key implementers whose input informs decisions; no veto, but can escalate. |
| **Informed (I)** | Those whose work may be impacted; receive updates and results. |
