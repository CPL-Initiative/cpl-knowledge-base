# Military Base CPL Demonstration Project

**Twentynine Palms (MCAGCC)  &  Copper Mountain College** — the embedded base/college demonstration within the California Community Colleges **Veterans Sprint**. A standalone strategic priority and the replicable template for extending CPL to California's military installations.

## Quick Summary

The Military Base CPL Demonstration is a direct extension of the Veterans Sprint — the Chancellor's Office initiative to ensure enrolled veterans, active duty, and reservists have their **Joint Services Transcripts (JSTs)** processed for college credit. It partners **MCAGCC Twentynine Palms** — the largest Marine Corps base in the country — with **Copper Mountain College (CMC)**, which already operates a dedicated on-base counseling office and has served active-duty service members for years.

Building on completed statewide credit recommendations and CMC's established student relationships, the project will upload JSTs for enrolled military students, award **Basic Training CPL** to every eligible veteran, and develop faculty-reviewed CPL pathways for the **42 Military Occupational Specialties (MOS)** trained at MCAGCC. The aim is a fully documented, **scalable model ready for replication across all 34 California military bases by March 2027**, serving as the evidentiary foundation for future state and federal funding requests and positioning the CCC system as the national leader in military CPL.

## Why Twentynine Palms and Copper Mountain College

Rated **Highest Impact + Readiness** for three reasons:

- **Scale** — The largest Marine Corps base in the country (~998 sq. miles, ~12,500 active-duty personnel). The Marine Corps Communications-Electronics School (MCCES) trains ~17,000 Marines annually across 42 MOS in 5 occupational fields, making each CPL articulation replicable at near-zero marginal cost.
- **Strong CPL alignment** — MCAGCC training maps directly to disciplines with existing statewide credit recommendations (CIS/Cybersecurity, Administration of Justice, EMT/Health Sciences, Early Childhood Education, Kinesiology, Automotive Technology, Foreign Languages), with significant additional opportunity in the 28xx Electronics MOS field — the highest-value new articulation not yet in the statewide library.
- **Established college partnership** — CMC maintains a dedicated on-base counseling office and an existing enrollment pipeline. The project adds CPL capacity to a functioning partnership; it does not start from scratch.

## Project Goals

The project pursues three goals over approximately two years.

1. **Evaluate and Articulate Military Training for CPL** — engage base leadership and CMC faculty to adopt applicable statewide credit recommendations, develop new CPL pathways where gaps exist, and upload JSTs for all enrolled and prospective service members.
2. **Develop and Revise CMC Programs to Maximize CPL** — engage CMC faculty to develop or revise courses and programs aligned with MCAGCC training, with MAP providing technical support (MOS crosswalks, draft equivalencies, alignment packages) so faculty time is scoped to review and approval, not independent research.
3. **Document, Disseminate, and Scale** — document outcomes, procedures, and partnerships for dissemination to the CCC field and military leadership; build MAP infrastructure for scalability; and establish data-sharing procedures to support state and federal funding requests.

## Funding (statutory and proposed)

- **IEPI Partnership Resource Team (PRT):** $250,000 full project funding.
- **MAP CPL Special Project:** additional personnel, technical resources, and CPL infrastructure.
- **Strong Workforce and Perkins IV:** CTE-aligned components.
- **Governor's proposed $35M local implementation fund:** performance-based, tied to CPL units transcribed.
- Demonstrated Year 1 outcomes will support CO leadership requests for additional federal and state investment.
