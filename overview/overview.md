# CPL Initiative & Veteran Sprint

## What this is

The Credit for Prior Learning (CPL) Initiative is the California Community College (CCC) Chancellor's Office (CO) systemwide effort to scale CPL across all 115 credit colleges, 4 noncredit campuses, and across all CPL types — military, industry certification, standardized assessment, and credit by exam or portfolio review. The Veteran Sprint, launched Fall 2024 by Chancellor Sonya Christian, is a focused effort to ensure that our colleges offer college credit to prospective service members and award credit to enrolled service members based on ACE and MAP the credit recommendations.

## The Veteran Sprint

**Objective:** The Veteran Sprint led by the California Community Colleges Chancellor’s Office in collaboration with the Mapping Articulated Pathways (MAP) team endeavors to accelerate how colleges recognize military learning through experience and coursework and award Credit for Prior Learning (CPL) for military students (veterans, active duty, reservists, national guard) as appropriate for their individual academic plan and pathway developed by their veteran counselor. (Current veteran-population target and JST-uploaded percentage: see dashboards.)

**Goals:**

- **Goal 1: Upload all enrolled military student JSTs into the MAP Platform. Note: Colleges that upload at least 75% of their MIS reported enrolled veterans are recognized with a star on the MAP Dashboard (Star College).
- **Goal 2: Award appropriate course credit for basic military training (commonly referred to as Basic Training or Boot Camp).
- **Goal 3: Offer additional CPL opportunities by articulating local college courses to JST ACE-listed and MAP-created Credit Recommendations (CRs). Underway statewide; relies on MAP's articulation library and faculty-validated AI matching.
- **Goal 4 — Engage in military base and college partnership demonstration project to ensure that regional college pathways are optimized to award credit for military base training. Underway statewide as Twentynine Palms and Copper Mountain College demonstration project.** A focused pilot at the Marine Corps Air Ground Combat Center (MCAGCC) at Twentynine Palms, in partnership with Copper Mountain College, covering 42 MOS pathways. Outcomes target **July 1, 2026**; the scalable model is intended to replicate across **34+ California military bases by March 2027**.

**Public progress cadence:** the Sprint reports against four named goals (JST upload, basic training credit, statewide JST articulation, military base demonstration project), each as a goal-to-date percentage. Current figures live on the public CPL Dashboard nd the CPL Initiative project tracker dashboard — see [`current-status/`](../current-status/README.md) a

## The broader CPL Initiative

The Veteran Sprint sits alongside two other concurrent sprints (Apprenticeship; Statewide Credit Recommendations) inside the multi-year CPL roadmap. The roadmap is anchored in **Vision 2030**, which sets a goal and a stretch goal for Californians receiving CPL by 2029-30 and a multi-year annual milestone schedule. Current goal-to-date progress, base goal, and stretch goal: [CPL Project Dashboard](../current-status/README.md).

**Three pillars structure the work:**

- **People** — embedded MAP-team support that functions as an extension of each college's CPL team.
- **Platform** — the MAP platform and its surrounding integrations (CCCApply, MIS, Credential Registry, Career Passport, Pathways Mapper). **Note - Culture is also referred to as Platform.
- **Policy** — accreditation alignment (ACCJC), faculty governance (ASCCC statewide credit recommendations), transfer (CSU/UC/private), federal compliance (VA, GI Bill), and state legislation

For the operating logic, see [Three-Pillar Initiative Design](../methodology/three-pillar-initiative-design.md).

## Funding

- **AB 123 (enacted 2025):** $5M/year ongoing + $15M one-time. First-ever ongoing state CPL funding.
- **FY2026 enacted budget:** additional $2M ongoing + $35M one-time.
- **ESS 25-82:** $50,000 implementation grants to all California Community Colleges, distributed in Spring 2026; certification surveys due January 15, 2026; funds fully expended by June 30, 2028. Three priority outcomes: JST processing, statewide credit-recommendation adoption, proactive CPL identification. See [ESS 25-82 funding memo](../policy-and-funding/ess-25-82-funding-memo.md).
